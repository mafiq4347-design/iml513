Lessons Stage 2: Condensation

☁️ Condensation

The second stage of the water cycle where clouds are born.

Phase: Gas to Liquid

What is Condensation?

Condensation is the process where water vapour in the atmosphere **cools down and changes back into tiny liquid water droplets**.

When billions of these microscopic droplets gather and float together high in the sky, they form the **clouds** that we see every day.

How Does It Happen?

1

Sun Creates Vapour

The Sun heats water surface bodies and produces warm water vapour.

2

Vapour Rises Up

The warm and light water vapour travels high up into the atmosphere.

3

Temperature Drops

As the air becomes cooler at higher altitudes, the water vapour loses heat energy.

4

Gas Turns to Liquid

The cooling effect changes the invisible gas back into tiny, visible liquid water droplets.

5

Clouds Form

Millions of these tiny droplets pack closely together to officially form a cloud.

Key Facts

🌡️ Temp drops

🌎 Needs for rain

Did You Know?

The water droplets inside clouds are extremely small! A single ordinary cloud contains millions of these microscopic droplets that float effortlessly in the air until they clump together and become heavy enough to fall.
